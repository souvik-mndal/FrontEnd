Mount Fuji, also known as Fuji-san, is Japan's highest and most iconic mountain, standing at 3,776 meters (12,389 feet). Located on Honshu Island, it is an active stratovolcano that last erupted in 1707. Revered in Japanese culture and art for centuries, Mount Fuji is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a popular destination for climbers and tourists. Its symmetrical cone shape and snow-capped peak make it a symbol of natural beauty and spiritual significance in Japan.
